MongoDB — Product Design Intern — Summer 2019

While interning on the Compass team at MongoDB, I learned that the design process isn't linear and it isn't sexy: it requires back-and-forths, constant iterating, and lots of patience. Through it all, I learned how to move through a product cycle, communicate with stakeholders, and design strategically. The hardest pill to swallow was that value-driven design is not always the prettiest.





I had the opportunity to work on two key projects:

  • a redesign of MongoDB Compass' connect screen as to allow for 1-click Atlas provisioning (seen above)
  • the implementation of a new toast component for MongoDB's design system
While I am unable to share the details of my work publicly, I'd be happy to talk about these projects privately. Feel free to reach out (kevintpham1231[at]gmail[dot]com) if you'd like to learn more.
