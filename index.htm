Dear visitor,

Welcome to my site—I'm Kevin (he/him). I'm a product designer at MongoDB and a graduate student at the CUNY Graduate Center. I currently reside in New York City.

I received my BA from UC Berkeley, where I studied Design Studies and New Media. While there, I focused my studies on the history and theory of digital media and design, and co-taught/started a course at the Berkeley Center for New Media titled "Beyond Design Thinking: Technology Through a Critical Lens." I was also involved in and .

I'm now pursuing an MA at the CUNY Graduate Center's Digital Humanities program. My research interests generally intersect between continental philosophy, critical race theory (especially Black critical theory), queer theory, computational media, and digital culture. Understood together, I'm interested in exploring the ways in which the afterlives of anti-black and racial violence condition philosophical and cultural engagements with technology. Questions I consider include: What are the relationships between anti-blackness, Western metaphysics, and digitality? How are desire, anxiety, and technology entangled? What does violence and resistance look like in digital spaces?

You can usually find me trying to figure things out and connect the dots on my Are.na in addition to my usual . More cohesive thoughts are articulated through my writing, which I plan to begin posting on here sometime in the near future.

While I consistently drop resources on Are.na, I also work together with some friends to collect BLM-related resources on a Notion workspace. The page is a growing compilation of resources shared by and produced for the Black Lives Matter movement: It includes lists of educational resources, places to donate, mutual aid networks, and much more. We also have some less-organized resources on an Are.na channel. I encourage you to check out these pages, and participate where you can in the movements for Black lives and police/prison abolition.

If you want to reach me, you can email me at kevintpham1231[at]gmail[dot]com. I'm particularly open to volunteering my time for any radical/activist organizations that need design, research, or writing assitance—I'd be glad to help out where I can.

Regardless of who you are, stay safe out there! I tend to update and play around with this site frequently as a way to procrastinate on other things, so I'll keep you posted.

With love,
Kevin
