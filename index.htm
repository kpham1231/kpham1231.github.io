Dear visitor,

Welcome to my site—I'm Kevin? (he/him). I'm a product designer at MongoDB and a graduate student at the CUNY Graduate Center. I currently reside in New York City.

I received my BA from UC Berkeley, where I studied Design Studies and New Media. While there, I focused my studies on the history and theory of digital media and design, and co-taught/started a course at the Berkeley Center for New Media titled "Beyond Design Thinking: Technology Through a Critical Lens." I was also involved in academic design research and design consulting.

I'm now pursuing an MA at the CUNY Graduate Center's Digital Humanities program. My research broadly engages black studies, critical and literary theory, affect theory, new media studies, and the history of technology. Understood together, I'm interested in exploring the ways in which the afterlives of anti-black violence condition philosophical and socio-political engagements with technology. Questions I consider include: What are the relationships between anti-blackness, Western metaphysics, and digital (humanist) practices? How are desire, anxiety, and digitality racialized and entangled? What does violence look like in (the production of) digital spaces?

You can usually find me trying to figure things out and connect the dots on my Are.na in addition to my usual reading.

If you want to reach me, you can email me at kevintpham1231[at]gmail[dot]com. I'm particularly open to volunteering my time for any radical/activist organizations—I'd be glad to help out where I can.

Regardless of who you are, stay safe out there! I tend to update and play around with this site frequently as a way to procrastinate on other things, so I'll keep you posted.

With love,
Kevin
